Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of how to search, organize, and extract knowledge from unstructured text. Topics include classical IR models, evaluation metrics, text mining techniques (classification, clustering, topic modeling), and neural approaches with transformers. Students will complete hands-on assignments and a semester project to develop scalable IR and text analysis systems.

Prerequisites

The course assumes familiarity with fundamental concepts in linear algebra, such as vector spaces, dot products, and matrix representations, since these underpin vector space models and similarity computations central to information retrieval. Students should also have a working knowledge of probability and statistics, including concepts like conditional probability, Bayes‚Äô rule, and distributions, which are essential for understanding probabilistic retrieval models and evaluation metrics. In addition, prior exposure to basic machine learning ideas‚Äîsuch as classification, clustering, and feature representation‚Äîwill help in grasping modern IR methods that integrate learning-based approaches. Proficiency in programming, particularly in Python, is also required, as students will implement retrieval algorithms, process text data, and experiment with libraries relevant to IR.

Course Policies

Late Submission Policy

Each student is allowed one opportunity during the semester to submit a deliverable late. A 20% penalty will be applied to that submission. After this one-time allowance, no further late submissions will be accepted.

Late work will not be accepted by email. Instead, the student must email the instructor, who will temporarily reopen the submission portal in Google Classroom to allow the late submission. Failure to follow this process will result in the submission not being graded.

Attendance Policy

Attendance will be recorded in every lecture. Students who miss more than 4 classes will incur a 5% deduction from their overall course grade.

Requests for leave of absence will not be granted for isolated or short-term emergencies. Leave will only be approved in cases where a student must be away for an extended period due to a genuine and unavoidable reason, subject to instructor approval and appropriate documentation.

Academic Integrity

All submitted work must represent your own effort unless collaboration is explicitly permitted. Plagiarism, unauthorized collaboration, or use of AI/online solutions without attribution is a violation of university policy and will result in disciplinary action, including a possible failing grade for the course.

Collaboration Policy

Students are encouraged to discuss high-level problem-solving strategies with classmates. However, all code, write-ups, and reports must be written individually. Copying or sharing solutions is not permitted. When collaboration is allowed, it will be explicitly stated in the assignment instructions.

Communication Policy

Piazza will be the primary platform for Q&A. Students should check regularly for announcements and clarifications. For personal matters (e.g., grades, accommodations), contact the instructor via email. TAs should be contacted during office hours for grading and assignment-related questions.

Disabilities & Accessibility Policy

Students with documented disabilities or special learning needs are encouraged to inform the instructor as early as possible in the semester so that appropriate accommodations can be arranged in coordination with the university‚Äôs disability services office.

All students are expected to meet the same learning objectives; however, reasonable adjustments will be made to ensure an inclusive and accessible learning environment. Confidentiality will be respected at all times.
